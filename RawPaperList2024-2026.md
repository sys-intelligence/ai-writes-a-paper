# Raw Paper List: AI/ML Security Papers at Top-4 Conferences (2024-2026)

## IEEE S&P 2024 (20 papers)

### AI/ML Security Papers

1. **Poisoning Web-Scale Training Datasets is Practical** — Shows practical attacks for poisoning web-scale datasets used to train ML models, demonstrating feasibility of data poisoning at internet scale. [Nicholas Carlini et al. (Google DeepMind, ETH Zurich)]

2. **Protecting Label Distribution in Cross-Silo Federated Learning** — Addresses privacy of label distributions in federated learning by developing protection mechanisms against distribution inference attacks. [Yangfan Jiang et al. (NUS)]

3. **FLShield: A Validation Based Federated Learning Framework to Defend Against Poisoning Attacks** — Defense against poisoning attacks in federated learning via validation-based approaches that verify client contributions. [Ehsanul Kabir et al. (Penn State)]

4. **Robust Backdoor Detection for Deep Learning via Topological Evolution Dynamics** — Uses topological data analysis to detect backdoor attacks in deep learning models by analyzing topological evolution during training. [Xiaoxing Mo et al.]

5. **Securing Graph Neural Networks in MLaaS: A Comprehensive Realisation of Query-based Integrity Verification** — Develops integrity verification mechanisms for GNN services in machine-learning-as-a-service platforms. [Bang Wu et al.]

6. **MEA-Defender: A Robust Watermark against Model Extraction Attack** — Watermarking defense mechanism against model extraction attacks with robustness guarantees. [Peizhuo Lv et al. (CAS)]

7. **SneakyPrompt: Jailbreaking Text-to-image Generative Models** — Demonstrates jailbreak attacks on text-to-image generative models by crafting adversarial prompts that bypass safety filters. [Yuchen Yang et al. (Johns Hopkins, Duke)]

8. **Transferable Multimodal Attack on Vision-Language Pre-training Models** — Develops cross-modal adversarial attacks on vision-language pre-training models with high transferability. [Haodi Wang et al. (Southeast University)]

9. **A Representative Study on Human Detection of Artificially Generated Media Across Countries** — Cross-country study examining human ability to detect AI-generated media across different cultural contexts. [Joel Frank et al. (Ruhr-Universität Bochum)]

10. **Moderating New Waves of Online Hate with Chain-of-Thought Reasoning in Large Language Models** — Uses LLM chain-of-thought reasoning for improved hate speech moderation and content safety. [Nishant Vishwamitra et al.]

11. **Please Tell Me More: Privacy Impact of Explainability through the Lens of Membership Inference Attack** — Demonstrates that model explainability increases vulnerability to membership inference attacks. [Han Liu et al. (WUSTL)]

12. **FlowMur: A Stealthy and Practical Audio Backdoor Attack with Limited Knowledge** — Develops stealthy backdoor attacks in the audio domain with minimal attacker knowledge requirements. [Jiahe Lan et al. (Xidian University)]

13. **AVA: Inconspicuous Attribute Variation-based Adversarial Attack bypassing DeepFake Detection** — Creates adversarial attacks that bypass deepfake detection systems through inconspicuous attribute variations. [Xiangtao Meng et al. (Shandong University)]

14. **Scores Tell Everything about Bob: Non-adaptive Face Reconstruction on Face Recognition Systems** — Demonstrates non-adaptive face reconstruction attacks exploiting score information from face recognition systems. [Sunpill Kim et al.]

15. **TROJANPUZZLE: Covertly Poisoning Code-Suggestion Models** — Introduces covert backdoor attacks on code suggestion models that are difficult to detect during training. [Hojjat Aghakhani et al. (UCSB, Microsoft)]

16. **SrcMarker: Dual-Channel Source Code Watermarking via Scalable Code Transformations** — Develops dual-channel watermarking for source code using scalable transformation techniques. [Borui Yang et al. (Shanghai Jiao Tong)]

17. **SMARTINV: Multimodal Learning for Smart Contract Invariant Inference** — Applies multimodal machine learning to infer invariants for smart contract security analysis. [Sally Junsong Wang et al. (Columbia)]

18. **From Individual Computation to Allied Optimization: Remodeling Privacy-Preserving Neural Inference with Function Input Tuning** — Novel approach to privacy-preserving neural inference through function input tuning optimization. [Qiao Zhang et al.]

19. **Hyena: Balancing Packing, Reuse, and Rotations for Encrypted Inference** — Optimizes encrypted ML inference by balancing packing, reuse, and rotation operations in homomorphic encryption. [Sarabjeet Singh et al. (University of Utah)]

20. **Understanding and Benchmarking the Commonality of Adversarial Examples** — Systematic study and benchmarking of adversarial example transferability and commonality across models. [Ruiwen He et al. (Zhejiang University)]

## IEEE S&P 2025 (17 papers)

### AI/ML Security Papers

1. **Query Provenance Analysis: Efficient and Robust Defense against Query-based Black-box Attacks** — This paper presents a defense mechanism against query-based black-box adversarial attacks on machine learning models by analyzing the provenance of queries to detect malicious probing. [Shaofei Li, Ziqi Zhang, Haomin Jia, Yao Guo, Xiangqun Chen, Ding Li]

[Junlin Wu, Jiongxiao Wang, Chaowei Xiao, Chenguang Wang, Ning Zhang, Yevgeniy Vorobeychik]

4. **Edge Unlearning is Not "on Edge"! An Adaptive Exact Unlearning System on Resource-Constrained Devices** — Proposes an efficient machine unlearning system for edge devices, addressing privacy concerns by enabling models to selectively forget specific training data. [Xiaoyu Xia, Ziqi Wang, Ruoxi Sun, Bowen Liu, Ibrahim Khalil, Minhui Xue]

5. **TSQP: Safeguarding Real-Time Inference for Quantization Neural Networks on Edge Devices** — Develops security mechanisms for quantized neural networks deployed on edge devices against adversarial attacks during real-time inference. [Yu Sun, Gaojian Xiong, Jianhua Liu, Zheng Liu, Jian Cui]

6. **Adversarial Robust ViT-based Automatic Modulation Recognition in Practical Deep Learning-based Wireless Systems** — Addresses adversarial robustness of Vision Transformer models for automatic modulation recognition in wireless communication systems. [Gen Li, Chun-Chih Lin, Xiaonan Zhang, Xiaolong Ma, Linke Guo]

7. **HARMONYCLOAK: Making Music Unlearnable for Generative AI** — Develops techniques to protect musical content from being learned by generative AI models, addressing intellectual property concerns in AI training data. [Syed Irfan Ali Meerza, Lichao Sun, Jian Liu]

8. **Supporting Human Raters with the Detection of Harmful Content using Large Language Models** — Explores the use of LLMs to assist human content moderators in detecting harmful content, addressing safety and scalability challenges in content moderation. [Kurt Thomas et al., Google]

9. **Watermarking Language Models for Many Adaptive Users** — Proposes watermarking techniques for language models to enable detection of AI-generated text in multi-user scenarios while maintaining model utility. [Aloni Cohen, Alexander Hoover, Gabe Schoenbach]

10. **Architectural Neural Backdoors from First Principles** — Investigates backdoor attacks that exploit the architecture of neural networks rather than training data poisoning, presenting a novel class of AI security threats. [Harry Langford, Ilia Shumailov, Yiren Zhao, Robert Mullins, Nicolas Papernot]

11. **Fight Fire with Fire: Combating Adversarial Patch Attacks using Pattern-randomized Defensive Patches** — Develops defense mechanisms against adversarial patch attacks using pattern randomization techniques to improve robustness of computer vision models. [Jianan Feng et al.]

12. **Towards Reliable Verification of Unauthorized Data Usage in Personalized Text-to-Image Diffusion Models** — Addresses privacy concerns in personalized text-to-image generation by developing methods to verify unauthorized usage of personal data in training diffusion models. [Boheng Li et al.]

13. **My Model is Malware to You: Transforming AI Models into Malware by Abusing TensorFlow APIs** — Demonstrates how AI models can be weaponized as malware delivery mechanisms by exploiting TensorFlow APIs, highlighting new attack vectors. [Ruofan Zhu, Ganhao Chen, Wenbo Shen, Xiaofei Xie, Rui Chang]

14. **CipherSteal: Stealing Input Data from TEE-Shielded Neural Networks with Ciphertext Side Channels** — Exploits side-channel vulnerabilities in TEE-protected neural network inference to extract sensitive input data through ciphertext analysis. [Yuanyuan Yuan et al.]

15. **BAIT: Large Language Model Backdoor Scanning by Inverting Attack Target** — Proposes novel methods for detecting backdoors in large language models by analyzing the inversion of attack targets during model training. [Guangyu Shen et al.]

16. **DataSentinel: A Game-Theoretic Detection of Prompt Injection Attacks** — Develops game-theoretic approaches to detect and mitigate prompt injection attacks against large language models. [Yupei Liu, Yuqi Jia, Jinyuan Jia, Dawn Song, Neil Zhenqiang Gong]
